# HIDDEN GEMS HALL OF FAME: DEAD BALL ERA (1901-1919)

## Elite Players NOT in the Hall of Fame Who Proved Their Character Through ACTION

**CRITERIA:** 
- Elite production (40+ WAR or equivalent)
- Documented acts of integrity, heroism, or service to others
- NOT in the Hall of Fame

---

## THE STARTING NINE

---

### LF or RF - JIMMY SHECKARD (Chicago Cubs)
**Career:** 1897-1913 | **WAR: 49.5** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- 2x World Series Champion (1907, 1908) with legendary Cubs
- Led NL in walks with 147 in 1911 (MLB record held for 9 years until Babe Ruth)
- Led NL in stolen bases 2x (1899, 1903)
- Led NL in home runs (1903), triples (1901), slugging (1901)
- First player EVER to hit inside-the-park grand slams in consecutive games
- Part of "Tinker to Evers to Chance" championship teams

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **During WWI, Sheckard served as Athletic Director at the Great Lakes Naval Training Station**, organizing baseball programs for thousands of servicemen. After baseball, he discovered future Cardinals star **Lester Bell** playing sandlot ball and personally contacted big league scouts to arrange his tryout. Bell said: **"Jimmy was a fine man. I may not have been a major league player if not for Jimmy."**

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- "Team leader who excelled at irritating the opposition" - played with fire
- Teammate Johnny Evers: "He was a bigger cog in the old invincible Cub machine than he ever received credit for being"
- Ring Lardner called him "the greatest ballplayer in the world"
- Sang baritone in a barbershop quartet with teammates
- Selected to All-Time Cubs team by Joe Reichler in 1962

### LF - BOBBY VEACH (Detroit Tigers)
**Career:** 1912-1925 | **WAR: 46.2** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- 3x AL RBI Champion (1915, 1917, 1918)
- Part of greatest outfield ever with Ty Cobb and Sam Crawford
- .310 career average, 2,063 hits
- Led AL in hits, doubles, AND triples in 1919
- More RBIs than ANY player in baseball from 1915-1922

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **Spotted Charlie Gehringer playing sandlot ball in 1923 and personally arranged his tryout with the Tigers.** Gehringer went on to become a Hall of Famer, 1937 MVP, and one of the greatest second basemen ever. Veach didn't have to do this - he was being pushed out by the Tigers at the time. He did it because it was the right thing for a young player.

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- Known as "too nice" for Ty Cobb, who tried to toughen him up
- "Easygoing, steady and unassuming" - SABR biography
- Worked coal mines at age 12 with his father
- When sold to Boston, Detroit fans gave him a diamond ring in gratitude
- Hit .382 at age 40 in minors - never stopped working

### 2B - LARRY DOYLE (New York Giants)
**Career:** 1907-1920 | **WAR: 45.1** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- 1912 Chalmers Award (NL MVP)
- 1915 NL Batting Champion (.320)
- 3x NL Pennant winner as team captain (1911-1913)
- First player to hit a home run out of the Polo Grounds

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **After his baseball career, Doyle dedicated years to working with the New York Police Athletic League, helping at-risk youth in New York City.** He gave back to his community when he didn't have to.

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- **"Laughing Larry"** - teammates elected him captain because they loved him
- "Larry was a gentleman, and he instilled that quality in all who surrounded him"
- Roommate of Christy Mathewson - "loved him like a brother"
- Famous quote: "It's great to be young and a New York Giant"
- Lived to 87, church packed at his funeral with "ordinary people who filled the ballparks"

---

### 3B - TOMMY LEACH (Pittsburgh Pirates)
**Career:** 1898-1918 | **WAR: 47.1** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- First World Series hit ever (triple off Cy Young, 1903)
- Still holds WS record with 4 triples in 1903
- Led NL in home runs (1902) - all 6 were inside-the-park
- 49 career inside-the-park HRs (2nd all-time)

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **Befriended deaf teammate Dummy Hoy and learned to communicate with him.** Leach never called for fly balls when playing next to Hoy because he knew Hoy couldn't hear him. "We got to be good friends. He was a real fine ballplayer."

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- Featured in *Glory of Their Times* at age 86 - still beloved
- "Wee Tommy" (5'6", 150 lbs) - played with heart bigger than his frame
- Lived to 91
- Bill James ranked him 20th best 3B ever

### SS - GRANT "HOME RUN" JOHNSON (Negro Leagues)
**Career:** 1895-1932 | **Hall Rating: 137** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- "Best everyday player in Black baseball from 1895 to 1909" - Gary Ashwill
- Hit .412 against Detroit Tigers in Cuba (outperformed Ty Cobb)
- Co-founder of National Association of Colored Professional Baseball Clubs
- Played until age 58

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **Nicknamed "Dad" by younger players because he mentored them throughout his career.** "He was a good instructor for young ballplayers, sharing valuable advice and helping them develop their skills."

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- Father served in Union Army's 13th Colored Infantry Regiment (Civil War)
- **"An honorable man of exemplary character"** - contemporaries
- Non-drinker, non-smoker his entire life
- Had a beautiful baritone singing voice
- Buried unmarked for 51 years until historians restored his grave (2014)
- Buffalo historian: "I believe this was a good man. He was not just a fabulous baseball player, he was a good man."

### CF - SPOTTSWOOD POLES (Negro Leagues)
**Career:** 1909-1923 | **Hall Rating: 66** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- **"The Black Ty Cobb"** - elite speed and hitting
- Batting averages: .440, .398, .414, .487 in peak years
- .594 average against major league competition
- John McGraw named him as one of 4 Black players he would have signed

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **At age 30, at the peak of his career, Poles enlisted to fight in WWI with the 369th Infantry Regiment (Harlem Hellfighters).** He earned a **Purple Heart** and **5 battle stars** for valor in combat. "I was proud then," he said of his service.

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- **Buried at Arlington National Cemetery** with full military honors
- Gave up prime earning years to serve his country
- Still eligible for HOF (2031 Early Baseball Era ballot)

### C - BRUCE PETWAY (Negro Leagues)
**Career:** 1906-1925 | **Hall Rating: ~40** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- Detroit Free Press: "probably one of the best catchers in the world"
- Connie Mack and John McGraw both called him best catcher in baseball
- Threw out Ty Cobb in Cuba (1910) - legendary moment
- Invented throwing to second base from a squat (still standard today)

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **Left Meharry Medical College - where he was studying to become a doctor - to pursue baseball.** After retiring, he mentored future Hall of Famers **Turkey Stearnes** and **Andy "Lefty" Cooper** as player-manager of the Detroit Stars.

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- "A smart ballplayer, the scrappy receiver was a student of the game"
- Played with his brother Howard as a battery
- "Elder statesman of the game" - reporters gathered to hear his stories
- Among first Black players on a baseball card (1909 Cabanas set)

### 1B - JAKE DAUBERT (Brooklyn/Cincinnati)
**Career:** 1910-1924 | **WAR: 39.4** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- 2x NL Batting Champion (1913, 1914)
- 1913 Chalmers Award (NL MVP)
- Captain of 1919 World Champion Reds
- .303 career average, 2,326 hits

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **When Daubert signed his first big contract, the first thing he did was tell his father to retire from the Pennsylvania coal mines after 57 years of labor.** He then supported his entire family with his baseball earnings.

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- **"Gentleman Jake"** - nickname for his character
- Started working coal mines at age 11 alongside his father
- Elected VP of Baseball Players Fraternity (replaced Ty Cobb)
- "Universally popular" - nominated for Brooklyn Alderman
- Book written about him: *Gentleman Jake* (2024)
- Deeply committed family man

### P - NAP RUCKER (Brooklyn Dodgers)
**Career:** 1907-1916 | **WAR: 47.1** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- 2.42 career ERA (4th best in Dodgers history)
- No-hitter in 1908 (14 K, 0 BB)
- Led NL in shutouts, complete games, innings pitched
- John McGraw called him best LHP of his era
- "Greatest .500 pitcher who ever lived" - stuck on terrible teams

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **Mentored young Casey Stengel when he was struggling.** "When I broke in I couldn't hit a low ball," Stengel recalled. "Nap took me to the park every morning and fed me low balls. If it hadn't been for him I reckon I wouldn't have even stayed in baseball."

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- "Gentlemanly Rucker" - known for his character
- After baseball, served as **Mayor of Roswell, Georgia** (1935-36)
- **Brought running water to his town** - served as water commissioner for decades
- Owner Charles Ebbets: "I will always regard him as one of the greatest men the game has produced. Greatest in every way."

---

### CF/Util - CLYDE MILAN (Washington Senators)
**Career:** 1907-1922 | **WAR: 40.1** | NOT IN HOF

**ELITE PLAYER:**
- "Deerfoot" - elite base stealer
- Led AL in steals 2x (1912-1913: 88 and 75)
- 495 career stolen bases
- Ty Cobb's close friend and roommate

**DOCUMENTED CHARACTER ACT:**
> **Clyde Milan died on the field.** On March 3, 1953, while serving as a coach for the Senators, he collapsed while hitting fungos to young players during spring training. He literally gave his last breath to the game, mentoring the next generation.

**Additional Character Evidence:**
- Well-respected enough that even Ty Cobb considered him a true friend
- Became Senators manager after playing career
- Dedicated his life to developing young players
